Dear Editor of *REGION*,

I am pleased to submit our manuscript entitled, "**Policy Attention versus Institutional Supply: How Local Governments Foster Cluster Resilience---A Mixed-Methods Case Study of China's Gaoyang Textile Industry**," for consideration in *REGION---The Journal of ERSA*.

**Fit with REGION**

Our paper sits at the intersection of regional economic resilience, institutional economics, and computational social science—all core interests of the ERSA community. *REGION*'s Diamond OA model and its commitment to publishing methodologically transparent regional science make it an ideal home for this work. The paper addresses a long-standing concern in regional studies: *how* local government actions translate into cluster-level resilience outcomes, moving beyond the "black box" treatment of institutions that characterizes much of the literature.

**Core Contribution**

We advance a conceptual distinction between *policy attention* (discursive emphasis measured via BERTopic dynamic topic modeling of 25 years of Chinese county government work reports) and *institutional supply* (actual resource allocation), and trace two micro-mechanisms—compliance cost socialization and transaction cost reduction—through which local governments foster cluster resilience. The empirical case is China's Gaoyang County, the world's largest towel production cluster (~1/3 of national output), which faced an existential environmental regulatory shock in 2017.

**Methodological Approach**

The paper integrates BERTopic neural topic modeling with Synthetic Control Method and Interrupted Time Series analysis in a transparent mixed-methods framework. We openly disclose all data constraints (including archival document length heterogeneity) and properly characterize quantitative patterns as *mechanism-illustrative* rather than causally confirmatory—an approach that prioritizes replicability and honesty over over-claiming.

**Key Features**

- First application of BERTopic to Chinese county-level government work reports for policy attention measurement
- Formal microeconomic model of compliance cost and transaction cost channels
- Two-stage resilience framework: baseline → value resilience
- Full transparency regarding single-case causal identification limits
- JEL codes: R11, R58, Q58, O25, C21
